## Definition

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is the build-up of atherosclerotic plaque in the epicardial coronary arteries, leading to reduced myocardial perfusion. It is the dominant underlying cause of [[concepts/Myocardial Infarction]] and a major driver of [[concepts/Heart Failure]] progression in the synthetic Pinewood Heart Center cohort.

## Mechanisms

- Atherosclerotic plaque formation in the vessel intima
- Plaque rupture or erosion leading to acute thrombosis
- Progressive luminal stenosis causing demand ischemia

## Presentation

Stable angina, unstable angina, and silent ischemia. Many cases are first detected at the time of an acute event.
