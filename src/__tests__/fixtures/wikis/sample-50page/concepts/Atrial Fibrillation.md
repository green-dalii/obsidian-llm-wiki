## Definition

Atrial Fibrillation (AF) is a supraventricular tachyarrhythmia characterized by uncoordinated atrial activation and consequent deterioration of mechanical function. In the synthetic Pinewood Heart Center cohort, AF is the dominant rhythm-disorder diagnosis driving anticoagulation adherence metrics per [[sources/Quality Dashboard]].

## Classification

- Paroxysmal (self-terminating within 7 days)
- Persistent (sustained beyond 7 days)
- Permanent (long-standing, no rhythm control attempts)
